2. Data Collection

We will be gathering our data in 3 parts. 1) Getting the data for when players got what injuries 2) Getting the data for player's statistics over 3) Scraping the internet to get the player's positions & how long they played

In each of the following sections we will explain both why we should be using this data and the format of the data.

2.1 Injury Data This is the main data that we are interested in. It was sourced from kaggle (https://www.kaggle.com/ghopkins/nba-injuries-2010-2018). The dataset has 5 columns 1) Date: What date the injury occurred 2) Team: Which team had this injry 3) Acquired: Who was acquired as a result of this injury 4) Reliquished: Who got hurt 5) Notes: A description of what had happened This data is important to us since it will give is us information regarding who injured.

2.2 Player Stats This is our independent data that we will be looking at to see if there is a relationship between it and injury. Specifically what we will be doing is taking an average accross all the years we have injury data for, and then preceed to see what conclusions could be drawn. This data comes from https://www.kaggle.com/justinas/nba-players-data. This Data has 19 collumns. This may seem like a lot, but rest assured we will only be using a subsection of this data. 1) Player_name: The name of the player 2) Team_abreviation: The team that the player belongs too 3) Age: the Age of the player at this particular year 4) Player_Height: The height of the player in cms 5) Player_Weight: The weight of the player in kgs 6) College: What College the player went too 7) Country: Which Country are they from 8) Draft_year: Which year were they apart of the rookie draft (not the same thing as their first year in the league as some people didn't get drafted directly to the NBA) 9) Draft_round: which round in the draft was hte player picked in 10) gp: Games Played 11) pts: Points per game 12) reb: rebounds per game 13) ast: average assits per game 14) net_rating: A team's point differential when this player is on the court 15) oreb_pct: Percentage of offensive rebounds grabbed 16) dreb_pct: Percentage of Defensive rebounds grabbed 17) usg_pct: Usage Percentage of player 18) ast_pct: percentage of teammates assisted while on the floor 19) season: What season are they playing in

#We are un ininterested in these 2 columns. Unnamed: 0 has no information in it and Acquired tells us people who the team brought in. We are only looking into players who got injured. 
df1 = df1.drop("Acquired", 1)
df2 = df2.drop("Unnamed: 0", 1)

#For the sake of sanity lets keep the column of our player name the same, so we do not get confused later 
df1 = df1.rename(columns={"Relinquished": "player_name"})

#while we are at it, lets drop any rows that dont have names associated with them. Though we know these players got injured, without their names we cant find the statsitcs surroudning their play thus they become useless. 
df1 = df1[pd.notnull(df1["player_name"])]

#If you uncommented df1.head() in the previous cell, you may have seen that the time information in both datasets are formatted differently. By creating a new column (year) for both datasets, we can more easily look up things we want
df2["year"] = df2["season"].apply(lambda x: x.split("-")[0])

#The astute among you may have noticed that the datasets for the player statistics covers 1996-2020. We only need the Data from from the 2011-2019 season. 
df2 = df2[(df2.year.astype(int) >= 2010) & (df2.year.astype(int) <= 2019)]

#This is a step feuled by hindisght. In later sections we are going to average several columns with respect to their years. 
#Doing this for age wouldn't really help us here (It would just be giving us the average age a player was in the NBA)
#A more interesting variable is the max age a player was before leaving the NBA. Here we are saving that under the dictionary age_list for later use 
age_list = df2.groupby(["player_name"])["age"].max()
df2 = df2.drop(["age"], axis = 1)

Its obvious from these results that the our regression wasn't the most accurate, however it is not a complete failure. Given the AUC score as well the resutls from our cross validation our model does an okay job predicting whether or not someone has injury. The overfitting does make sense since so many statistics like net-rating is a function of the other variables.
